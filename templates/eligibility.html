About Blood Donation

Blood donation is a voluntary act where individuals donate blood for medical use. It supports surgeries, trauma care, chronic illnesses, and more. The first recorded blood transfusion in India took place in 1939, and since then, it has become a vital part of healthcare.

Types of Blood & Compatibility

  • A: Has A antigen; Anti-B antibodies
  • B: Has B antigen; Anti-A antibodies
  • AB: Has A & B antigens; Universal recipient
  • O: No antigens; Universal donor

Rh factor further classifies into Positive and Negative types.

Q. How often can I donate blood?
Whole blood: every 56 days. Platelets: every 7 days. Plasma: every 28 days.
Q. Will I feel weak after donating?
Most donors feel fine. Slight fatigue may occur but rest, hydrate, and eat iron-rich foods.
Q. Is blood donation healthy?
Yes! Reduces iron overload, improves circulation, and helps detect hidden health issues.
Q. What should I do before donating?

Q. What should I eat after donating?
